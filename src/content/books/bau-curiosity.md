David Bau defends the scientific view of mechanistic interpretability, arguing that we are in a "Copernican moment" for the study of rational thought. He uses the history of the telescope to illustrate how curiosity-driven science—pointing our lenses at the stars rather than just ships on the horizon—is essential for understanding what it truly means to think.
